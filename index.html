Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,848.86 meters, located on the border between Nepal and Tibet in the Himalayas. Known as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, it attracts climbers from all over the world. The first successful ascent was made by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953. Due to its extremely cold weather, low oxygen levels, and dangerous terrain, climbing Everest is considered one of the toughest mountaineering challenges.
